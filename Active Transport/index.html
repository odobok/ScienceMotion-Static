Active Transport

Active transport is the movement of dissolved molecules into or out of a cell through the cell membrane, from a region of lower concentration to a region of higher concentration.

Scroll to see how particles move against the concentration gradient.

Sometimes molecules need to move from low to high concentration. Or against the concentration gradient

In order to get those molecules to the higher concentration, you need energy.

Carrier proteins take those molecules and use energy released during respiration to transport the molecules across the membrane.

With the energy and the channel that the molecules travel through, antiporter, the molecules arrive at the higher concentration!
